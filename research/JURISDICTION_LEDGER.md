# Cross-jurisdiction evidence ledger

Reviewed: 2026-07-15. Primary sources should be rechecked before use.

| Jurisdiction | Primary source | What it supports | What it does not establish |
|---|---|---|---|
| Australia | [TGA clinician tapering sheet](https://www.tga.gov.au/resources/publication/publications/clinician-information-sheet-opioid-analgesic-tapering) | Patient-specific plans, monitoring and support; complex cases may need specialist input. | That every long-term prescription should continue or that one patient meets an exception. |
| Australia | [TGA consumer information](https://www.tga.gov.au/resources/explore-topic/prescription-opioids-hub/prescription-opioids-information-consumers-patients-and-carers) | Do not stop suddenly; discuss gradual and safe reduction with a health professional; long-term benefits and harms must be assessed. | A right to a requested medicine or dose. |
| Australia | [Australian Charter of Healthcare Rights](https://www.safetyandquality.gov.au/your-rights) | Access, safety, respect, partnership, information, privacy, and feedback. | A case-specific finding of negligence, illegality, or prescribing obligation. |
| United Kingdom | [NICE NG215](https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng215/chapter/Recommendations) | Usually avoid abrupt stopping; use slow stepwise reduction; modify schedules for intolerable withdrawal; document and offer a second opinion when agreement cannot be reached. | That patient preference compels a prescriber to continue treatment believed not to be in the person's best interests. |
| United Kingdom | [GMC Good medical practice](https://www.gmc-uk.org/professional-standards/the-professional-standards/good-medical-practice) | Work in partnership and support informed decisions. | A civil or regulatory judgment about a particular encounter. |
| United States | [CDC 2022 opioid guideline](https://www.cdc.gov/mmwr/volumes/71/rr/rr7103a1.htm) | Avoid rapid taper/abrupt discontinuation absent life-threatening danger; collaborate, individualize, avoid abandonment, and arrange care. | An inflexible standard, guaranteed continuation, or legal entitlement. |
| United States | [HHS tapering guide](https://www.hhs.gov/system/files/Dosage_Reduction_Discontinuation.pdf) | Individualized, generally slow tapering; pauses may be appropriate; longer prior therapy may require longer tapering. | A universal schedule or patient-specific treatment order. |

## Cross-national comparison rule

Do not rank an entire national system from one case. Comparable system-level
research needs a shared outcome definition, sampling frame, denominator, policy
context, and documented counterexamples.
